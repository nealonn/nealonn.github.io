ABOUT LA CHELITA

A Tex-Mex diner rooted in family, heritage, and community.

Our Story

La Chelita began in the 1970s as La Tapatía, a small family-owned diner serving affordable, homemade Jalisco-style meals to the East Austin community. Decades later, Jessica Ramirez—granddaughter of the original founder, Doña Chelita—has lovingly reimagined the restaurant while honoring its family traditions.

Today, La Chelita blends authentic flavors, Jalisco heritage, and modern Austin spirit. Every dish still carries the warmth, pride, and heart that inspired the very first menu.

Meet the Owner

Jessica
Ramirez

Owner & Chef

Jessica is a third-generation restaurateur and proud Mexican-American chef from Texas. She grew up in her grandmother Chelita’s kitchen learning recipes, stories, and the importance of feeding a community.

Our Values

Authenticity: Everything we do is rooted in Jalisco flavors and family recipes.

Community: We are a gathering place for longtime locals and new neighbors alike.

Warmth & Hospitality: We treat every guest like family—just like Chelita did.

Freshness: Real ingredients, handmade dishes, bold flavors.

The La Chelita Experience

When you enter La Chelita, you’re stepping into a space filled with color, music, and the aroma of home-cooked food. Talavera tiles, vintage family photos, and warm lighting create an inviting atmosphere—where traditions live on and new memories are made.

Community Focus

From longtime neighborhood regulars to new residents discovering us for the first time, La Chelita is built for everyone. We believe in creating a space where cultures meet, stories are shared, and community thrives.

“La Chelita isn’t just a restaurant — it’s a tribute to family, flavor, and the people who keep our community strong.”
